What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is the psychological manipulation of people to persuade them to reveal confidential information or perform actions that compromise security.

Instead of attacking computers directly, attackers target human emotions such as fear, trust, curiosity, and urgency.

🎯 Common Social Engineering Techniques

  • Urgency: "Your account will be locked in 30 minutes."
  • Fear: "Your computer has been infected."
  • Authority: Pretending to be a manager, police officer, or bank employee.
  • Curiosity: "See who viewed your profile."
  • Greed: "Congratulations! You've won a new smartphone."

📖 Real-Life Scenario

You receive a phone call from someone claiming to be from your bank. They say there has been suspicious activity on your account and ask you to confirm your account number and one-time password (OTP).

Although the caller sounds professional, legitimate banks never ask for your password or OTP over the phone.

🚨 Warning Signs

  • Pressure to act immediately.
  • Requests for passwords or OTPs.
  • Unexpected phone calls or messages.
  • Offers that sound too good to be true.
  • Requests to keep the conversation secret.

✅ Key Takeaways

  • Think before you trust.
  • Never share passwords or OTPs.
  • Verify the person's identity independently.
  • Stay calm under pressure.
  • Report suspicious calls and messages.
