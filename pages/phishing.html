What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where criminals pretend to be a trusted person, company, or organization to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, credit card numbers, or personal information.

The attacker may use fake emails, fake websites, text messages, phone calls, or social media messages to deceive victims.

🎯 Why Do Attackers Use Phishing?

  • Steal usernames and passwords
  • Gain access to bank accounts
  • Steal personal identity information
  • Install malware or ransomware
  • Access company systems

📌 Common Types of Phishing

📧 Email Phishing

The most common type where attackers send fake emails pretending to be companies like banks, PayPal, Amazon, or Microsoft.

🎯 Spear Phishing

A highly targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific individual or organization using personalized information.

📱 Smishing

Phishing attacks delivered through SMS or text messages.

☎️ Vishing

Attackers make fake phone calls pretending to be bank staff, government officials, or technical support.

📄 Clone Phishing

Attackers copy a legitimate email and replace links or attachments with malicious ones.

⚠️ How a Phishing Attack Works

  1. The attacker creates a fake email or website.
  2. The victim receives the message.
  3. The victim clicks the malicious link.
  4. The victim enters sensitive information.
  5. The attacker steals the information.

💡 Example Scenario

Imagine receiving an email saying:

Your bank account has been locked.

Click here immediately to verify your account or it will be permanently suspended.

The email looks genuine and contains the bank's logo, but the link actually leads to a fake website designed to steal your login credentials.

✅ Key Takeaways

  • Never trust unexpected emails asking for personal information.
  • Always verify the sender.
  • Check website URLs carefully.
  • Think before clicking any link.
  • When in doubt, contact the organization directly.
