Why Phishing Emails Work

Cybercriminals design phishing emails to look like messages from trusted organizations such as banks, online shopping sites, universities, or government agencies. Their goal is to make you panic or act quickly without thinking.

🚩 Warning Signs of a Phishing Email

  • Unknown or suspicious sender email address
  • Urgent messages like "Your account will be closed today!"
  • Poor spelling and grammar mistakes
  • Unexpected attachments
  • Requests for passwords or banking information
  • Links that don't match the official website
  • Generic greetings such as "Dear Customer"

Example of a Phishing Email

From: support@paypaI-security.com

Subject: Urgent! Verify Your Account

Dear Customer,

We detected unusual activity on your account. Click the link below immediately to verify your account. Failure to do so within 24 hours will permanently suspend your account.

Verify Now

What's Wrong With This Email?

  1. The sender's email address is fake.
  2. It creates unnecessary urgency.
  3. It uses a generic greeting.
  4. It asks you to click a suspicious link.
  5. It tries to scare you into acting quickly.

✅ How to Stay Safe

  • Never click suspicious links.
  • Check the sender's email carefully.
  • Hover over links before clicking them.
  • Contact the company using its official website.
  • Report phishing emails to your email provider.
  • Delete suspicious emails immediately.
