Why Cyber Safety Matters

Many phishing attacks succeed not because of advanced technology, but because victims are unaware of common warning signs. Following a few simple security practices can significantly reduce your risk of becoming a victim.

✅ Best Practices

  • Use strong and unique passwords for every account.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) whenever possible.
  • Never share your passwords or One-Time Passwords (OTPs).
  • Always verify website URLs before entering sensitive information.
  • Avoid clicking links from unknown emails or text messages.
  • Keep your operating system and software up to date.
  • Use trusted antivirus and security software.
  • Back up important files regularly.

🚫 Things You Should Never Do

  • Don't reuse the same password across multiple websites.
  • Don't open unexpected email attachments.
  • Don't trust offers that seem too good to be true.
  • Don't enter personal information on suspicious websites.
  • Don't ignore browser security warnings.
